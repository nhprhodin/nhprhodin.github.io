Research Interests

Welcome to my research page. I am an observer at heart, and spend most of my time extracting information encoded in astronomical images taken with world-class ground- and space-based telescopes. My current research focuses on galaxies selected via their gas cross section towards background quasars. This technique avoids bias towards the most luminous “tip of the ice-berg” accessed by conventional methods (luminosity selected samples), and is believed to probe a more representative portion of the gas-rich galaxy population. In particular, I want to understand the conditions for star formation; the metal-enrichment of the circum-galactic medium; and which sight-lines through galaxies cause damped neutral Hydrogen (HI) absorption (Damped Lyman-α absorption (DLAs) and Sub-DLAs).
